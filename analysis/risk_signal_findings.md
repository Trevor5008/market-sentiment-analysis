## Findings
Based on the results of extended lag and aggregation analysis:
- Extended lag and aggregation analysis suggests that sentiment does **not** provide reliable short-term predictive signal for returns.
- Extreme sentiment events exhibit asymmetric behavior. 
    - Negative sentiment retains statistical significance under permutation testing and aligns with downside movements, while positive sentiment appears largely explained by underlying market drift. 
### Quantifyibg Asymmetric Risk Signal
- To validate the conditional risk hypothesis, sentiment was measured into
deciles.

### Results
- Bottom decile (most negative sentiment) exhibits:
    - Lower average returns relative to baseline
    - Elevated forward volatility
    - Statistically significant deviation under permutation testing
- Top decile (most positive sentiment):
    - Does not show statistically signficant performance
    - Effects appear consistent with underlying market drift

### Summary
- Results suggest that sentiment may function more effectively as a **conditional risk indicator** rather than a symmetric return predictor.
